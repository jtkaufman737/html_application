Resolving Customer Qualms

At times, I have had to diplomatically remind customers of their Service Level Agreements when they have asked for services or man hours that were not part of a contract or agreement. I will be frank, it is never simple to tell people things they don't want to hear. But by being objective, providing concrete examples, active listening, and demonstrating empathy I have been able to reach understanding.

Is the customer always right?

I am a logical person, and have to say no. It is statistically impossible to be right 'all of the time'. I believe it is possible to know that and simultaneously realize that the saying really should be, 'The customer is always first'.

Whether right or not, customers are the heart of your company. Their troubles and concerns are paramount to the health of your organization. So right or not, customers are first. This means truly listening to them before recommending solutions. Instilling confidence that you take their issue seriously, and will be pulling out all the stops to resolve it. And then finalizing the feedback loop: confirming what you think is a solution is seen that way by the customer too.
